Research

My research lies at the intersection of networking, learning, and systems for intelligent decision-making. I am especially interested in how constrained devices, edge servers, and cloud resources can be coordinated to deliver practical and reliable AI.

Hierarchical and Cascade AI

Modern AI systems often need to decide whether to answer locally or to offload computation to a stronger edge or cloud model. My work studies the algorithmic foundations of hierarchical inference, including confidence-aware routing, decision modules, queueing effects, and performance tradeoffs across the device-edge-cloud hierarchy.

  • Hierarchical Inference with an Offload Queue, INFOCOM 2026.
  • Exploring the Boundaries of On-Device Inference: When Tiny Falls Short, Go Hierarchical, IEEE IoT Journal 2025.
  • Getting the Best Out of Both Worlds: Algorithms for Hierarchical Inference at the Edge, IEEE TMLCN 2024.

Inference Offloading and Edge Systems

I study algorithms that determine when to execute models on-device, when to use an edge server, and when to escalate to the cloud. The goal is to jointly optimize latency, communication, energy, accuracy, and monetary cost rather than treating them as separate design dimensions.

  • Inference Offloading for Cost-Sensitive Binary Classification at the Edge, AAAI 2026.
  • Offloading Algorithms for Maximizing Inference Accuracy on Edge Device Under a Time Constraint, IEEE TPDS 2023.

Age of Information and Freshness

Another focus of my work is information freshness in distributed sensing and monitoring systems. I am interested in how stale information affects control, estimation, and decision-making in networked environments.

  • 2D-AoI: Age-of-Information of Distributed Sensors for Spatio-Temporal Processes, IEEE TCOM 2025.

Online Learning and Networked Decision Systems

I also work on online learning, stochastic optimization, queueing, and decision-making problems that arise in adaptive systems. These tools support learning-based routing, hierarchical inference, and broader resource allocation in networks and information systems.

  • Online Learning with Stochastically Partitioning Experts, UAI 2025.
  • Regret Bounds for Online Learning for Hierarchical Inference, MobiHoc 2024.

Current Themes

  • Reliable and cost-efficient Edge AI for real deployments.
  • Routing and orchestration across heterogeneous models.
  • Freshness-aware sensing and monitoring at scale.
  • Learning and control under communication and compute constraints.
